A2 Milk Cow Farm Subsidy

50% Government Support for Indigenous Cattle Farms in AP

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. A2 Milk Cow Farm Subsidy

Category: Policy Analysis • Updated: February 2026 • Reading Time: 10 minutes

The Andhra Pradesh government is promoting A2 milk production through indigenous cattle farming with a 50% subsidy up to ₹15 lakhs. This initiative supports farmers in setting up profitable desi cow farms while preserving native breeds and producing healthier milk.

What is A2 Milk?

A2 milk comes from indigenous (desi) cow breeds that produce milk with the A2 beta-casein protein. This is different from A1 milk produced by most commercial (exotic/crossbred) cattle.

Why A2 Milk is Preferred

  • Easier Digestion: Many people who are lactose intolerant can digest A2 milk
  • Health Benefits: Associated with fewer digestive issues
  • Premium Pricing: A2 milk commands 50-100% higher prices
  • Growing Demand: Health-conscious consumers actively seeking A2 milk

Government Subsidy Details

Subsidy Structure

Component Amount
Government Subsidy 50% of project cost
Maximum Subsidy Up to ₹15 lakhs
Bank Loan 40-45% (subsidized interest)
Beneficiary Contribution 5-10%

Indigenous Breeds Promoted

  • Ongole: Native to AP, excellent for milk and draft
  • Punganur: Small breed, high-quality A2 milk
  • Krishna Valley: Good milk yield and disease resistance
  • Gir: High A2 milk producer
  • Sahiwal: Premium milk breed
  • Red Sindhi: Heat-tolerant, good milk yield

Joint Liability Groups (JLG) Model

The government encourages farmers to form Joint Liability Groups (JLGs) for collective dairy farming:

Benefits of JLG

  • Easier Credit: Banks prefer group lending
  • Shared Risk: Collective responsibility reduces individual burden
  • Economies of Scale: Bulk purchase of feed, veterinary services
  • Better Marketing: Collective milk marketing power
  • Knowledge Sharing: Learn from each other's experiences

How to Form JLG

  1. Form a group of 4-10 farmers interested in dairy farming
  2. Approach your local RBK or NABARD office
  3. Complete JLG registration
  4. Open joint bank account
  5. Apply for dairy scheme as JLG

How to Apply

Step 1: Visit RBK

  • Go to your nearest Raithu Bharosa Kendra
  • Meet the Animal Husbandry Assistant
  • Express interest in A2 milk cow farming

Step 2: Prepare Documents

  • Aadhaar card
  • Land documents (for shed construction)
  • Bank account details
  • Passport photographs
  • JLG registration (if applying as group)

Step 3: Submit Application

  • Fill the application form
  • Submit business plan/project proposal
  • Indicate number of cows and breed preference
  • Specify infrastructure requirements

Step 4: Bank Loan Processing

  • Application forwarded to bank
  • Bank verification and approval
  • Subsidy component credited directly
  • Loan disbursement begins

Project Components Covered

  • Purchase of Indigenous Cows: High-quality breeding animals
  • Shed Construction: Modern, ventilated cattle housing
  • Milking Equipment: Hygienic milking machines
  • Fodder Development: Green fodder cultivation
  • Cold Storage: Milk chilling equipment
  • Working Capital: Initial feed and veterinary costs

Training & Support

The government provides:

  • Training Programs: Indigenous cattle management, breeding, health care
  • Veterinary Support: Regular health camps and vaccinations
  • Fodder Development: Guidance on green fodder cultivation
  • Marketing Support: Linking with dairy cooperatives and processors

Eligibility

  • Farmers, landless laborers, SHG members
  • Age 18-60 years
  • Space for cattle shed
  • Commitment to maintain indigenous breeds
  • JLG membership preferred for higher benefits

Contact

  • RBK: Your nearest Raithu Bharosa Kendra
  • Animal Husbandry Helpline: 1962
  • NABARD: District office for JLG guidance
  • District Dairy Officer: For scheme details
