How Community Bulk Ordering Works

  1. Register as Community Coordinator
    The apartment secretary or a volunteer registers on Jaikisan's Community Orders platform. They become the liaison between residents and farmers.
  2. Collect Requirements from Residents
    Using WhatsApp groups or the Jaikisan app, the coordinator collects weekly vegetable/fruit requirements from families. A simple Google Form or WhatsApp poll works great.
  3. Consolidate & Place Bulk Order
    All individual requirements are combined into one bulk order. The larger the combined quantity, the higher the discount tier unlocked for everyone.
  4. Farmers Deliver to Community Gate
    On the scheduled day (usually weekly), farmers deliver directly to the apartment gate. The coordinator distributes to individual families based on their orders.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum order quantity?

For community bulk orders, we recommend a minimum combined order of 25kg to unlock the first tier of discounts. However, you can start with smaller quantities to test the service.

How does payment work?

The coordinator collects payments from individual families and makes a consolidated payment to the farmer. Payment can be made via UPI, bank transfer, or cash on delivery.

What if there's a quality issue?

We guarantee freshness. If any produce doesn't meet quality standards, report within 24 hours for replacement or refund. Our farmers are committed to quality because they want repeat orders.

Can we choose our delivery day?

Yes! Communities can choose their preferred delivery day (typically Saturday or Sunday for residential apartments). The schedule is fixed for consistency.

Do you deliver to all areas?

Currently, the community bulk ordering program is available in Hyderabad, Vijayawada, Visakhapatnam, Guntur, Tirupati, and Warangal. More cities are being added monthly.
