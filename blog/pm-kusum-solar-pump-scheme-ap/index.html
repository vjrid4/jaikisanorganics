PM-KUSUM Solar Pump Scheme AP

Solarize Your Agriculture - Component C Guide 2026

Category: Policy Analysis • Updated: February 2026 • Reading Time: 10 minutes

Andhra Pradesh is aggressively solarizing agriculture through the PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan) scheme. The state has set a target to solarize 1.36 lakh existing grid-connected agricultural pump sets under Component C, with a completion deadline of March 31, 2026.

What is PM-KUSUM?

PM-KUSUM is a central government scheme to promote solar energy in agriculture. It has three components:

Component A: Solar Power Plants

Setting up 10,000 MW of decentralized solar power plants on barren/agricultural land.

Component B: Standalone Solar Pumps

Installation of 20 lakh standalone solar-powered agriculture pumps.

Component C: Solarization of Grid-Connected Pumps (AP Focus)

Solarization of 15 lakh existing grid-connected agriculture pumps. This is the main focus in AP.

Component C: How It Works

Under Component C, farmers with existing electric pumps can add solar panels to:

  • Use solar power for irrigation during daytime
  • Sell excess power to DISCOMs at pre-determined rates
  • Reduce electricity dependence and contribute to grid stability
  • Earn additional income from power sales

Benefits for Farmers

1. Free/Subsidized Solar Panels

  • Central Government: 30% subsidy
  • State Government: 30% subsidy
  • Farmer Contribution: Only 40% (can be financed)

2. Income from Excess Power

  • Sell surplus solar power to DISCOM
  • Fixed rate agreements for power purchase
  • Additional income ₹3,000-8,000/month possible

3. Reduced Electricity Bills

  • Use free solar power for irrigation
  • No dependence on erratic grid supply
  • Irrigation anytime sun is available

4. Environmental Benefits

  • Clean, renewable energy
  • Reduced carbon footprint
  • Contribution to national solar goals

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must have an existing grid-connected agricultural pump
  • Pump should be in working condition
  • Valid agricultural electricity connection in farmer's name
  • Land ownership documents required
  • Space available for solar panel installation

How to Apply

Step 1: Check Eligibility

  • Verify your pump is grid-connected
  • Confirm connection is in your name
  • Assess space for solar panels (roof/ground)

Step 2: Visit RBK or DISCOM Office

  • Go to your nearest Raithu Bharosa Kendra
  • Or visit local APSPDCL/APEPDCL office
  • Express interest in PM-KUSUM Component C

Step 3: Submit Application

  • Fill application form
  • Submit required documents:
    • Aadhaar card
    • Land documents
    • Electricity bill/connection details
    • Bank account details

Step 4: Site Survey

  • Technical team will visit your farm
  • Assess solar installation feasibility
  • Determine panel capacity needed

Step 5: Installation

  • Pay your 40% contribution (or arrange loan)
  • Empanelled vendor installs solar system
  • Grid connection and net metering setup
  • System commissioning and handover

Financial Details

Component Percentage For 5HP System (~₹2.5L)
Central Subsidy 30% ~₹75,000

Financing Options: Banks offer loans for farmer's 40% share at subsidized interest rates. EMI can be offset against power sale income.

Income Potential

Example: 5 HP Solar System

  • Solar capacity: ~3.5-4 kW
  • Daily generation: 15-18 units
  • Self-use for irrigation: 5-8 units
  • Surplus sold to grid: 7-10 units
  • At ₹3/unit: ₹21-30 per day
  • Monthly income: ₹600-900
  • Annual income: ₹7,000-11,000

⏰ Deadline: March 31, 2026

AP government has set a strict target to complete solarization of 1.36 lakh pumps by March 2026. Apply early to ensure inclusion in this phase.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (farmer)
  • Agricultural land documents (Pattadar passbook/1-B)
  • Existing electricity connection proof
  • Recent electricity bill
  • Bank passbook (for subsidy credit)
  • Passport size photographs
  • Mobile number (Aadhaar-linked)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get solar if I have free electricity?

A: Yes. Even with free agricultural power, you can benefit by selling excess solar power to the grid.

Q: What if I don't have ₹1 lakh for my share?

A: Banks provide loans for farmer contribution. The monthly income from power sales can cover EMI.

Q: Who maintains the solar panels?

A: Vendors provide 5-year comprehensive maintenance. After that, minimal maintenance needed (cleaning panels).

Q: What about monsoon months?

A: Generation reduces during cloudy/rainy days. You can use grid power when needed. No power sale income during low-generation periods.
