YSR Pasu Nashta Parihara Padhakam

Livestock Loss Compensation Scheme for AP Farmers

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Livestock Compensation

Category: Policy Analysis • Updated: February 2026 • Reading Time: 8 minutes

YSR Pasu Nashta Parihara Padhakam (Livestock Loss Compensation Scheme) provides financial relief to farmers who lose their cattle, sheep, goats, or other livestock. This is a cornerstone welfare scheme that protects farmers from economic devastation due to animal deaths.

How to Claim Compensation

⚠️ Critical: Report Within 72 Hours

Claims must be made immediately following the loss. Report to your local RBK within 72 hours for valid claim processing.

Step 1: Report the Loss Immediately

  • Contact the Animal Husbandry Assistant at your nearest RBK
  • Or call the village veterinary officer
  • Do this within 24-72 hours of the animal's death
  • Do NOT dispose of the carcass before verification

Step 2: Veterinary Verification

  • Veterinary officer will visit and examine
  • Post-mortem may be conducted if required
  • Death certificate will be issued
  • Cause of death will be documented

Step 3: Submit Claim Documents

  • Death certificate from veterinary officer
  • Aadhaar card of the farmer
  • Animal registration/tagging details (if available)
  • Bank passbook (for compensation credit)
  • Photographs of the animal (if available)

Step 4: Claim Processing

  • RBK forwards claim to District Animal Husbandry Office
  • Verification and approval process
  • Compensation credited to bank account
  • Timeline: 15-30 days typically

Eligibility

  • Farmer/animal owner must be AP resident
  • Animal should be registered (tagging preferred)
  • Death due to disease, accident, or natural causes
  • Report made within stipulated time

Not Eligible For

  • Slaughter or intentional killing
  • Death reported after 72 hours
  • Animals without proper documentation
  • Commercial farms (different provisions)

2025-26 Focus: Indigenous Breed Promotion

The government is currently emphasizing promotion of indigenous cattle breeds alongside compensation:

Local Breed Cow Farms (A2 Milk Initiative)

  • 50% subsidy (up to ₹15 lakhs) for setting up indigenous cow farms
  • Focus on A2 milk production
  • Joint Liability Groups (JLGs) encouraged
  • Training and support through RBKs

For more on this, see our A2 Milk Cow Farm Subsidy Guide.

Jagananna Paala Velluva

Another complementary scheme providing milch animals to SHG women farmers:

  • Free/subsidized milch animals to women SHG members
  • Focus on boosting milk production
  • Ongoing for 2025-26 cycle
  • Apply through your local RBK

Tips for Farmers

1. Register Your Animals

Get your livestock tagged/registered at the RBK. This makes claim processing faster and smoother.

2. Keep Records

Maintain photographs and purchase records of your animals. Useful for verification.

3. Know Your RBK Contact

Save the contact number of your local Animal Husbandry Assistant. In emergencies, quick contact is crucial.

4. Don't Delay

Report losses immediately. Delays beyond 72 hours may result in claim rejection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is this scheme always active?

A: Yes, this is an always-active scheme. Claims can be made year-round whenever livestock loss occurs.

Q: What if my animal died at night?

A: Report first thing in the morning. The 72-hour window allows for such situations. Do not dispose of the carcass.

Q: Can I claim for multiple animals?

A: Yes, each animal loss can be claimed separately with proper documentation.
