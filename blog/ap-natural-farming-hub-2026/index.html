What is Natural Farming?

Natural farming, as practiced under APCNF, eliminates synthetic chemicals and relies on:

  • Jeevamrutham: Microbial soil activator from cow dung and urine
  • Beejamrutham: Natural seed treatment
  • Mulching: Soil cover to retain moisture and feed soil life
  • Crop Diversity: Multi-cropping for ecological balance

How to Join Natural Farming

Step 1: Express Interest

  • Visit your nearest RBK
  • Talk to the Village Agriculture Assistant
  • Join the local natural farming farmer group

Step 2: Attend Training

  • Participate in village-level training sessions
  • Learn to prepare Jeevamrutham, Beejamrutham
  • Understand pest management with natural methods

Step 3: Start on One Plot

  • Begin with a portion of your land
  • Apply Jeevamrutham every 15 days
  • Start mulching practices
  • Gradually expand as you gain confidence

Step 4: Get Certified

  • Join PGS group for certification
  • Document your farming practices
  • Peer verification by fellow farmers
  • Receive PGS India certification

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will my yields drop permanently?

A: No. Initial dip is temporary. By year 3-4, most farmers report yields equal to or better than chemical farming, with much lower input costs.

Q: Where do I get desi cow for Jeevamrutham?

A: Any cow's dung and urine works. You can share resources with neighbors or participate in village-level bio-input preparation.

Q: Can I get premium prices for natural produce?

A: Yes, with PGS certification. Many urban consumers and organic retailers pay 20-50% premium for verified natural produce.
