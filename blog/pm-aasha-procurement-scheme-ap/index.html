PM-AASHA: MSP Procurement Scheme

100% Procurement of Pulses & Oilseeds at Minimum Support Price

  2. Blog

Category: Policy Analysis • Updated: February 2026 • Reading Time: 10 minutes

PM-AASHA (Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay SanraksHan Abhiyan) is the Government of India's flagship scheme for ensuring remunerative prices to pulse and oilseed farmers through guaranteed MSP procurement. For 2025-26, the scheme is active throughout the financial year with 100% procurement of eligible produce.

What is PM-AASHA?

PM-AASHA was launched to address the concerns of pulse and oilseed farmers who often face price crashes below MSP during harvest seasons. The scheme has three components:

1. Price Support Scheme (PSS)

  • Direct physical procurement of pulses, oilseeds, and copra at MSP
  • Implemented by NAFED and other central agencies
  • 100% procurement guarantee for registered farmers
  • No quantity limit for eligible farmers

2. Price Deficiency Payment Scheme (PDPS)

  • Difference between MSP and selling price paid directly to farmer
  • Farmer can sell in open market
  • DBT transfer of price difference to bank account
  • Currently operational in select states

3. Private Procurement & Stockist Scheme (PPSS)

  • Private players procure at MSP on behalf of government
  • 15% service charge paid to private agencies
  • Expands procurement reach in remote areas

Eligibility Criteria

  • Registered Farmers: Must be registered on e-Panta/state portal
  • Land Records: Valid pattadar passbook or land ownership documents
  • Crop Registration: Crop should be registered during sowing season
  • Quality Standards: Produce must meet FAQ (Fair Average Quality) norms
  • No Quantity Limit: Entire registered quantity eligible for MSP

How to Sell Under PM-AASHA

Step 1: Register Your Crop

  • Register on e-Panta portal before crop maturity
  • Enter crop details, expected yield, and land particulars
  • Get registration acknowledgment

See our e-Panta Registration Guide for step-by-step instructions.

Step 2: Watch for Procurement Announcements

  • NAFED announces procurement windows via SMS
  • Check AP Markfed/state agency announcements
  • Procurement centers notified district-wise

Step 3: Bring to Procurement Center

  • Visit designated NAFED/state procurement center
  • Carry Aadhaar, pattadar passbook, bank passbook
  • Registration acknowledgment from e-Panta

Step 4: Quality Testing & Weighment

  • Produce tested for moisture content and quality
  • FAQ (Fair Average Quality) standards apply
  • Weighment at certified scales

Step 5: Payment via DBT

  • MSP amount credited directly to bank account
  • Payment within 48-72 hours of procurement
  • SMS confirmation of payment

Procurement Centers in AP

PM-AASHA procurement is conducted through:

  • NAFED Centers: Primary procurement agency for pulses
  • AP Markfed: State-level marketing federation
  • FCI Godowns: In select locations
  • Primary Agricultural Credit Societies: Village-level procurement
  • Rythu Bharosa Kendras: One-stop farmer service centers

Benefits for Farmers

Price Protection

  • Guaranteed MSP: No selling below support price
  • Market Crash Protection: Secure income even in glut
  • 100% Procurement: No quantity rejection

Telangana Implementation

In Telangana, PM-AASHA operates with similar guidelines:

  • Registration: Through Dharani portal and local RBKs
  • Procurement Agency: NAFED + Telangana Markfed
  • Major Crops: Tur, Bengal Gram (Chana), Groundnut
  • Payment: DBT to Aadhaar-linked bank accounts

Sell Beyond MSP on Jaikisan

While PM-AASHA guarantees MSP, premium quality pulses and oilseeds can fetch higher prices through direct buyer connections. List your produce on Jaikisan Marketplace to connect with traders, processors, and exporters who pay premium prices for quality produce.

Common Issues & Solutions

Rejected for Quality

Solution: Ensure proper drying (below 12% moisture), clean foreign matter, and store in dry conditions. Re-bring after meeting FAQ standards.

No SMS About Procurement

Solution: Visit local Rythu Bharosa Kendra or check AP Markfed website for procurement schedule.

Long Waiting at Centers

Solution: Reach early morning. Token system usually operates - get token and wait for turn. Some centers accept online slot booking.
