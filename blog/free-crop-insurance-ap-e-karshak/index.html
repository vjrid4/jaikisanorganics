What is AP Free Crop Insurance?

The Andhra Pradesh government has made crop insurance completely free for farmers. Under this scheme:

  • Zero Premium: Farmers pay nothing - government bears full premium
  • Automatic Enrollment: All e-Panta registered farmers are covered
  • Comprehensive Coverage: Natural calamities, pests, diseases, weather events
  • Multiple Schemes: PMFBY for yield-based, WBCIS for weather-based coverage

Types of Crop Insurance Available

1. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Yield-based insurance that covers crop losses:

  • Coverage based on actual yield vs guaranteed yield
  • Claims settled through Crop Cutting Experiments (CCE)
  • Covers standing crops, post-harvest losses, localized calamities
  • Premium: 2% Kharif, 1.5% Rabi (fully paid by AP Govt)

2. Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme (WBCIS)

Weather parameter-based insurance for specific crops:

  • Triggers based on weather parameters (rainfall, temperature, humidity)
  • Automatic claims - no field assessment needed
  • Quick settlement through weather data
  • Ideal for crops sensitive to weather variations

How to Register for Free Crop Insurance

Step 1: Complete e-Panta Registration

Free crop insurance requires e-Panta registration. Once you register your crops on e-Panta, you're automatically enrolled for insurance.

Step 2: Verify on e-Karshak Portal

  1. Visit the e-Karshak portal or use the app
  2. Login with your Aadhaar-linked mobile number
  3. Check "Insurance Status" section
  4. Verify your crop and coverage details

Step 3: Keep Documents Ready

  • e-Panta registration acknowledgment
  • Land documents (Pattadar passbook)
  • Bank account details
  • Photographs of crop (in case of claims)

Crops Covered Under Free Insurance

Kharif Crops

  • Paddy, Maize, Jowar, Bajra
  • Groundnut, Cotton, Soybean
  • Redgram, Greengram, Blackgram
  • Chillies, Turmeric

Rabi Crops

  • Paddy, Maize, Jowar
  • Bengal gram, Sunflower
  • Groundnut, Tobacco

Horticultural Crops (WBCIS)

  • Mango, Banana, Papaya
  • Citrus fruits
  • Coconut, Oil Palm

How to File Insurance Claims

For Crop Damage/Loss

  1. Report Immediately: Inform your local RBK within 72 hours of damage
  2. Document Damage: Take clear photographs of affected crops
  3. File Claim: Submit claim through e-Karshak portal or RBK
  4. Field Visit: Insurance/agriculture officials will assess damage
  5. Claim Settlement: Amount credited directly to bank account

Timeline for Claims

Coverage and Compensation

What is Covered?

  • Natural Calamities: Floods, droughts, cyclones, hailstorm
  • Pests & Diseases: Crop damage due to pest attacks
  • Prevented Sowing: If you couldn't sow due to adverse conditions
  • Mid-Season Adversity: Damage during crop growth
  • Post-Harvest Losses: Damage within 14 days of harvest
  • Localized Calamities: Hailstorm, landslide in specific areas

What is NOT Covered?

  • Damage due to negligence or poor farming practices
  • Theft or malicious damage
  • War, nuclear risks
  • Unregistered crops

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need to pay any premium?

A: No. The AP government pays 100% of the premium. It's completely free for farmers.

Q: Am I automatically covered if I registered on e-Panta?

A: Yes, e-Panta registration automatically enrolls you for crop insurance.

Q: How do I know if my crop is insured?

A: Check your insurance status on the e-Karshak portal or visit your RBK.

Q: What documents do I need for claims?

A: e-Panta acknowledgment, land documents, damage photographs, and bank details.
